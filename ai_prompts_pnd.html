AI Prompting Guide for Policy & Research Professionals

A Workshop Companion

Author

Workshop Materials

Published

Introduction

This guide provides structured prompts for professionals in policy analysis, economic research, data science, and public administration. Each prompt follows best practices for effective AI interaction.

How to Use This Guide

  1. Select the category relevant to your task
  2. Customize the prompt with your specific details
  3. Iterate based on results
  4. Refine for better outputs

Part 1: Prompt Engineering Fundamentals

The XML Structure Method

Basic Template

<role>You are a [specific expert role]</role>
<task>[Clear, actionable task]</task>
<context>[Background information]</context>
<output_format>
- [Format requirement 1]
- [Format requirement 2]
</output_format>

Part 2: Policy Analysis & Evaluation

Punjab Climate Change Policy Evaluation

Simple Evaluation Framework

You are a public policy analyst.
Evaluate the Punjab Climate Change Policy I am sharing.

Provide assessment in this structure:

1) **Summary** (150 words)
   - Key goals & focus areas

2) **Strengths**
   - Institutional strength
   - Sector coverage
   - Implementation tools

3) **Gaps/Weaknesses**
   - Execution challenges
   - Financing constraints
   - Data & capacity issues

4) **Feasibility Score** (0-10) with justification

5) **Quick Wins** (Top 5 actions for next 12 months)

6) **Risks & Mitigation** (bullet points)

7) **Urdu Brief** (5 lines for citizens)

Keep it practical, Punjab-context focused, administrative tone.
Focus on execution, realism, and citizen impact.

Audience-Specific Policy Summaries

  • Secretaries/Senior Bureaucrats
  • Students (Policy & Governance)
  • Journalists/Media
  • Parliamentarians
  • Local Government Officials
  • Farmers
  • Civil Society/NGOs

Extract the top execution risks and 5 most urgent administrative actions to operationalize the Punjab Climate Change Policy in the next 6 months.

Summarize the Punjab Climate Change Policy in simple language, identify main policy tools, and explain how it affects citizens, farmers, and cities.

Provide a neutral, fact-based media brief: key objectives, priority actions, expected benefits, and accountability mechanisms.

Translate the policy into 10 talking points focusing on public benefits, district-level delivery, and budget oversight.

Extract district-level responsibilities and list top 5 actions each district and tehsil should take immediately.

Explain agriculture and water-related parts in simple Urdu and list 5 ways farmers will benefit.

Identify how civil society can support awareness, community preparedness, tree campaigns, and climate-smart livelihood programs.

Constitutional Amendment Analysis

Comprehensive Amendment Evaluation Prompts

**1. Key Provisions Summary**
Summarize key provisions of the Constitution (Twenty-sixth Amendment) 
Act, 2024, and explain how it amends Pakistan's Constitution.

**2. Legislative Process Analysis**
Analyze the legislative process in National Assembly and Senate, 
including key debates and voting outcomes.

**3. Objectives Assessment**
What were the primary objectives and how does it address specific 
issues in Pakistan's governance or judicial system?

**4. Political Dynamics**
Discuss political dynamics surrounding passage, including support 
and opposition from major parties (PML-N, PPP, PTI).

**5. Judicial Impact**
Evaluate potential impact on Pakistan's judiciary, particularly 
regarding judicial appointments and accountability.

**6. Comparative Analysis**
Compare with previous amendments (19th, 21st) in terms of scope 
and impact.

**7. Long-term Implications**
Predict long-term effects on Pakistan's democratic institutions 
and rule of law.

Part 4: Data Analysis & Visualization

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Basic Data Inspection

**Initial Dataset Review:**

1. "Show summary statistics for [list key variables]"

2. "What is the structure of the dataset? Describe types and 
   counts of each column."

3. "Display first few rows and identify any immediate data quality issues."

4. "Calculate mean, median, standard deviation for each numeric column."

**Missing Data Analysis:**

1. "Identify columns with missing values and percentage of missingness."

2. "Visualize missing data pattern in the dataset."

3. "Suggest appropriate imputation methods for missing values."

**Outlier Detection:**

1. "Detect outliers using IQR method and Z-score."

2. "Plot boxplots for key numerical columns to inspect outliers visually."

3. "Determine if outliers should be removed or retained based on context."

Descriptive Statistics

**Comprehensive Statistical Summary:**

Generate a comprehensive statistical report including:

1. Central tendency measures (mean, median, mode)
2. Dispersion measures (std dev, variance, range, IQR)
3. Distribution shape (skewness, kurtosis)
4. Correlation matrix for numerical features
5. Frequency distributions for categorical variables
6. Cross-tabulations for key variable combinations

Identify and flag:
- Highly skewed distributions
- High correlation pairs (r > 0.7)
- Variables with low variance

Machine Learning Workflows

Model Building Framework

**Complete ML Pipeline:**

1. **Data Preparation**
   - Split data into training (80%) and test (20%) sets
   - Apply cross-validation strategy
   - Handle class imbalance if present

2. **Feature Engineering**
   - Create interaction terms where appropriate
   - Apply polynomial features for non-linear relationships
   - Use PCA for dimensionality reduction if needed

3. **Model Training**
   - Train [specify model type] to predict [target variable]
   - Compare at least 3 different algorithms
   - Tune hyperparameters using grid search/random search

4. **Model Evaluation**
   - For classification: accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, AUC-ROC
   - For regression: RMSE, MAE, R-squared
   - Generate confusion matrix/residual plots
   - Perform cross-validation evaluation

5. **Interpretation**
   - Show feature importances
   - Generate SHAP values for explainability
   - Create partial dependence plots

Part 5: Text & Document Analysis

Monetary Policy Statement Analysis

Exploratory Text Analysis

**1. Word Frequency Analysis**
"What are the most common words in monetary policy statements?"
"List most frequent bigrams and trigrams used in statements."
"Identify unique terms differentiating one statement from another."

**2. Sentiment Analysis**
"Analyze overall sentiment (positive, negative, neutral) in each statement."
"Identify trends in sentiment over time."
"Perform comparative sentiment analysis between different periods."
"Identify sentences with highest positive/negative sentiment scores."

**3. Topic Modeling**
"What are main topics discussed? Use topic modeling to identify themes 
like inflation, employment, growth."
"Analyze prevalence of key themes over time."
"Track changes in topics before/after major economic events."

**4. Economic Terms Tracking**
"Track frequency of key terms ('inflation,' 'unemployment,' 
'interest rates') over time."
"Determine relationship between word frequency and actual 
economic indicators."

**5. Policy Tone Analysis**
"Identify tone (cautious, optimistic, concerned) of each statement."
"Compare tone of statements after interest rate changes vs. 
steady rate periods."

**6. Uncertainty Language Detection**
"Identify language expressing uncertainty ('may,' 'likely,' 'possibly')."
"Extract predictive statements indicating potential future actions."
"Compare uncertainty levels before/after major economic events."

   - Implementation tools

   - Execution challenges
   - Financing constraints
   - Data & capacity issues

5) **Quick Wins** (Top 5 actions for next 12 months)

6) **Risks & Mitigation** (bullet points)

7) **Urdu Brief** (5 lines for citizens)

Keep it practical, Punjab-context focused, administrative tone.
Focus on execution, realism, and citizen impact.

  • Secretaries/Senior Bureaucrats
  • Students (Policy & Governance)
  • Journalists/Media
  • Parliamentarians
  • Local Government Officials
  • Farmers
  • Civil Society/NGOs

Provide a neutral, fact-based media brief: key objectives, priority actions, expected benefits, and accountability mechanisms.
