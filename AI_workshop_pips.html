AI Workshop for Punjab Government Policy Officers

Generative AI and Agentic AI for Public Policy Transformation

Author

Zahid Asghar

Published

November 06, 2025

Workshop Overview

ImportantTarget Audience

Public Policy Officers, Decision Makers, and Government Officials from Punjab Government

This intensive workshop introduces Punjab Government officials to cutting-edge Generative AI and Agentic AI technologies that can deliver exponential productivity gains in public policy work. Participants will learn to leverage AI agents, automated workflows, and advanced prompt engineering to transform how government operates‚Äîmoving from traditional manual processes to AI-amplified decision-making systems.

The workshop emphasizes practical implementation of AI systems that can analyze complex policy scenarios, generate comprehensive reports in minutes, and create intelligent workflows that operate autonomously to support governance objectives.

TipWorkshop Duration

Full Day Intensive Workshop - Approximately 8 hours including breaks

0.1 Enhanced Learning Objectives

0.1.1 1. Master Agentic AI for Government Operations

  • Understanding AI agents that can perform complex, multi-step policy analysis autonomously
  • Implementing AI workflows that operate 24/7 to monitor policy impacts and generate insights

0.1.2 2. Deploy 1000x Productivity Multipliers

  • Learn AI systems that can process vast amounts of legislative data, public feedback, and economic indicators simultaneously
  • Create AI-powered dashboards that provide real-time policy impact assessments

0.1.3 3. Advanced AI Tool Mastery

  • Hands-on experience with latest AI platforms: Claude Sonnet 4, GPT-4, specialized government AI tools
  • Multi-modal AI applications (text, data visualization, document analysis)
  • AI-powered code generation for data analysis without programming skills

0.1.4 4. Strategic AI Integration

  • Design AI governance frameworks for responsible deployment
  • Create sustainable AI adoption strategies specific to Punjab Government needs

policy_data <- data.frame(

p <- plot_ly(policy_data, x = ~Month, y = ~Implementation_Score, 
             type = 'scatter', mode = 'lines+markers', 
             name = 'Implementation Score') %>%
  add_trace(y = ~Public_Satisfaction, name = 'Public Satisfaction') %>%
  add_trace(y = ~Budget_Efficiency, name = 'Budget Efficiency') %>%
