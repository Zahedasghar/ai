The Evolution of AI Systems

Traditional LLMs

  • Launch: November 2022 (ChatGPT)
  • Thinking: “System 1” - Fast, intuitive
  • Capabilities: Language generation, single-pass responses
  • Limitations: No reflection, no real-time data

Reasoning Models

  • Launch: September 2024
  • Thinking: “System 2” - Deliberate, step-by-step
  • Capabilities: Complex math, formal proofs, debugging
  • Trade-offs: Slower, more expensive

Agentic Chatbots

  • Launch: December 2024
  • Thinking: Autonomous planning + tool use
  • Capabilities: Multi-step workflows, external tools
  • Power: End-to-end research automation

AI Agent Architecture

Key Difference from Chatbots: Agents can autonomously decompose tasks, gather information from multiple sources, execute code, and iteratively refine their approach based on intermediate findings.

Breakthrough Agent Applications

Deep Research Systems

  • Multi-agent Architecture: Orchestrator spawns specialized sub-agents for parallel investigation
  • Scale: Process 100+ sources in minutes, literature reviews in under an hour
  • Synthesis: Compile findings into coherent reports with accurate citations

Claude Code (“Vibe Coding”)

  • Natural Language Programming: Create software from English descriptions
  • Speed: Build econometric tools in minutes rather than hours
  • Accessibility: No programming expertise required

Current Limitations: Still require human oversight, prone to hallucinations, struggle with genuine economic reasoning at researcher level

What Economists Can Do Today

Literature Reviews

Automated scanning and synthesis of hundreds of sources with proper citations

Code Generation

Write and debug econometric code, implement estimation procedures

Data Analysis

End-to-end workflows: download, clean, analyze, visualize economic data

Custom Tools

Build interactive research applications and specialized agents

Building Your Own Research Agents

LangGraph Framework

# Simple agent architecture
workflow = StateGraph(AgentState)
workflow.add_node("think", think_node)
workflow.add_node("act", act_node)
workflow.add_node("observe", observe_node)  
workflow.add_node("respond", respond_node)

# Define flow
workflow.add_edge("think", "act")
workflow.add_edge("act", "observe")
workflow.add_edge("observe", "respond")

Key Components:

  • State Management: Centralized state object flows through entire graph
  • Nodes: Each function operates on the state
  • Dynamic Routing: Conditional edges enable adaptive behavior
  • Parallel Processing: Multiple agents can work simultaneously

Recursive Truth: AI agents are now building AI agents - the tools help create more sophisticated successors

Open Protocols for Agent Interoperability

Model Context Protocol (MCP)

  • Universal standard for connecting agents to resources and data
  • 10,000+ servers available (as of writing)
  • Enables access to FRED, IMF, institutional databases
  • Like “USB-C for AI agents”

Agent2Agent (A2A) Protocol

  • Enables AI agents to communicate with each other
  • Agent Cards describe capabilities and connections
  • Enables specialized agents to collaborate
  • Industry deployment in customer service, automation

Research Implications: A lead research agent could connect to multiple data sources through MCP, then delegate subtasks to specialized agents (data cleaning, financial analysis, econometric modeling) that communicate via A2A protocols.

Economic and Strategic Considerations

Pricing Evolution

  • Premium Subscriptions: $200+ per month (10x increase from standard $20)
  • Potential Future: PhD-level scientist systems at $20,000/year
  • Access Inequality: Advanced capabilities increasingly function of ability-to-pay

Countervailing Forces

Open Source Models: DeepSeek, Alibaba’s Qwen offer near-frontier capabilities at lower costs

Competition: Kimi-K2 offers GPT-4 performance at 1/100th the price

Strategic Advice: Maintain flexibility across model providers rather than committing to a single ecosystem

Current Limitations & Challenges

Technical Limitations

  • Hallucinations: Generate plausible but incorrect content
  • Computational Cascades: Errors propagate through multi-agent workflows
  • Brittleness: Small prompt variations can cause failures
  • Prompt Injection: Vulnerable to malicious manipulation

Economic Research Specific

  • Limited Economic Reasoning: Misapply theoretical frameworks
  • Training Data Bias: Reproduce common misconceptions
  • Not Independent Researchers: Require human oversight

Best Practice Analogy: “Treat AI agents like research assistants: they require careful planning, oversight during execution, and detailed vetting of results.”

Future Implications for Economics

Capability Spiral

AI agents help build better AI agents, suggesting accelerating pace of development

Near-term Opportunities (Today)

  • Junior researchers can accomplish what once required entire teams
  • Technical barriers dissolving - democratization of research capabilities
  • More ambitious research agendas become feasible

Long-term Transformation

  • Economist’s role shifts from producing analysis to defining values and interpreting implications
  • Research generation becomes increasingly automated
  • Focus on uniquely human aspects: ethical reasoning, creative problem formulation, wisdom about social welfare

Special Responsibility: Economists understand incentives, market dynamics, and resource allocation - crucial insights for AI-augmented research ecosystem

Key Takeaways

We’ve Crossed a Critical Threshold

Economists can now build sophisticated research assistants without technical expertise, conduct autonomous literature reviews, and create analytical tools through natural language alone.

Practical Recommendations

  • Embrace Tools Now: Build your own agents to understand capabilities and limitations
  • Invest in Human Skills: Ethical reasoning, creative problem formulation, social welfare wisdom
  • Guide Transformation: Help shape AI-augmented research ecosystem
  • Maintain Oversight: Current agents are superb assistants, not independent researchers

The Stakes: How we integrate AI agents into economic research will influence how society understands and addresses its most pressing challenges. Our task is to ensure that as artificial colleagues grow more capable, our human contribution grows more thoughtful, ethical, and focused on human well-being.
